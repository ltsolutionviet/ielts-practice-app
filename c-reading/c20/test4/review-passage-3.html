Questions 27–31: Matching Information

27. an example of how one predator has been protected by the introduction of livestock guard dogs.

Answer: D

Paragraph D clearly refers to cheetahs in Namibia, which were often killed because they were believed to attack livestock. With the presence of guard dogs, 90% of farmers reported reduced livestock losses and were less likely to kill predators, particularly cheetahs.
→ This is a clear example of a predator being protected through the use of livestock guard dogs.

28. an optimistic suggestion about the possible positive developments in the use of livestock guard dogs.

Answer: G

Paragraph G presents an optimistic outlook that guard dogs may not only reduce conflicts between humans and wildlife but could also help protect biodiversity, for example by protecting birds from foxes.
→ This suggests a positive future potential for the use of guard dogs.

29. a description of how the methods used by livestock guard dogs help to keep predators away.

Answer: B

Paragraph B describes how guard dogs operate: they grow up with livestock, treat them as part of their family, position themselves between the herd and predators, bark loudly, and if necessary chase predators away.
→ This paragraph gives a detailed description of the dogs’ protective methods.

30. claims by different academics that the use of livestock guard dogs is a successful way of protecting farmers' herds.

Answer: C

Paragraph C provides research evidence from Silvia Ribeiro (Portugal) and Linda van Bommel (Australia) showing that guard dogs are highly effective in reducing or even eliminating livestock losses caused by predators.
→ Several academics claim that guard dogs are very successful in protecting livestock.

31. a reference to how livestock guard dogs gain their skills.

Answer: B

Paragraph B explains that guard dogs are raised with livestock from a very young age, which leads them to see the animals as their family.
→ This describes how they develop the skills needed for their protective role.

Questions 37–40: Summary Completion (ONE WORD ONLY)

Unintended ecological effects of using guard dogs

In Namibia, livestock guard dogs have been used to protect domestic animals from attacks by cheetahs.
This has led to a rise in the deaths of other predators, particularly jackals.
In addition, it has been suggested that the dogs could carry diseases, which may affect other species, and that they may reduce the amount of food available to certain wild animals.
On the other hand, these dogs may help birds by protecting their nests, which might otherwise be threatened by predators such as foxes.
