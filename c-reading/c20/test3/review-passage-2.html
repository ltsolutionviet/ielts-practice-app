✅ Questions 14–19: Matching Headings

Choose the correct heading for each paragraph A–F.

14. Paragraph A → v. Two clear educational goals

Explanation:
Paragraph A discusses the introduction of an artificial coral reef at London Zoo with two objectives:

  • Showing the public the beauty and importance of coral reefs (educational goal 1).
  • Highlighting research and conservation efforts aimed at saving coral reefs (educational goal 2).

15. Paragraph B → ii. Cooperation beneath the waves

Explanation:
Paragraph B describes the symbiotic relationship between coral and algae. The algae live inside the coral and provide nutrients, while the coral protects the algae → this represents "cooperation beneath the waves".

16. Paragraph C → iv. Disagreement about the accuracy of a certain phrase

Explanation:
Paragraph C mentions that some people describe coral reefs as “rainforests of the sea,” but David Attenborough disagrees with this description → indicating a disagreement about the accuracy of the phrase.

17. Paragraph D → vii. A warning of further trouble ahead

Explanation:
This paragraph describes serious threats such as rising temperatures, ocean acidification, and pollution. It also warns that 90% of coral reefs could disappear by 2050 → a clear warning about future problems.

18. Paragraph E → iii. Working to lessen the problems

Explanation:
Paragraph E describes research aimed at identifying stronger coral species and accelerating artificial reproduction → efforts designed to reduce the impact of problems such as climate change.

19. Paragraph F → vi. Promoting hope

Explanation:
Paragraph F mentions global research cooperation and the presentation of research results to the public, demonstrating that there is still hope for saving coral reefs → promoting optimism and awareness.
