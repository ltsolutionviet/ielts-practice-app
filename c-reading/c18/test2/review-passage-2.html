Explanation for Questions 14–19 (Multiple choice)

14. Answer C.
Why: The opening paragraph (beginning with “This has been the decade of AI…” and discussing “narrow AI”, then stating: “many experts believe this restriction is very temporary. By mid-century, we may have artificial general intelligence (AGI)…”) shows that the author believes the current limitation of AI (performing only specialised tasks) may be temporary. In other words, AI capabilities are expected to expand in the future. Therefore, the main idea is that there is no reason to believe AI abilities will remain limited → choose C.
Eliminating other options:
A (difficulty predicting the speed of progress): not the main focus; the paragraph emphasises expanding capabilities.
B (learning from chess machines): the chess example only illustrates narrow progress and is not the paragraph’s main point.
D (experts disagree about specialised tasks): the paragraph says experts believe the limitation is temporary, not that they disagree.

15. Answer A.
Why: The next paragraph explains why machines could surpass humans: machines are not constrained by the physical limitations of the human brain (speed, size) and can design even more intelligent machines. Therefore, the paragraph explains why machines may surpass humans → A is correct.
Elimination: B (describing similarities) is incorrect because the paragraph emphasises differences; C (providing general information about development) is too vague; D is not relevant.

16. Answer B.
Why: The author introduces the story of King Midas immediately after stating “we must specify goals with great care” and writes: folklore…people who ask for the wrong thing, with disastrous consequences — King Midas…. The purpose is to illustrate that poorly defined goals can lead to disastrous outcomes. Therefore B (poorly defined goals may go wrong) is correct.
Elimination: A, C, and D do not match the purpose of the Midas example in the paragraph.

17. Answer D.
Why: The paragraph states: “One thing that makes this task difficult is that we are far from reliably human-friendly ourselves. We do many terrible things… If superintelligent machines don’t do a lot better than us, we’ll be in deep trouble.” The author emphasises that the challenge lies in the fact that humans themselves are not consistently ‘human-friendly’. Therefore it is difficult to ensure machines behave better than humans ethically. This corresponds to ensuring AI is more humane than humans → D.
Elimination: B (choosing which values to share) is somewhat related but the paragraph mainly emphasises human inconsistency; A and C do not match the paragraph.

18. Answer C.
Why: The paragraph states: “we want the machines to be ethically as well as cognitively superhuman… If there are routes to the moral high ground, they’ll be better than us at finding them.”. This indicates that the author predicts machines may surpass humans in certain respects (for example, morally or intellectually). Therefore C (“Machines will be superior to humans in certain respects”) fits best.
Elimination: A (safety will be an issue) may be true for the whole article but not the specific focus of this paragraph; B and D do not match the paragraph’s idea.

19. Answer D.
Why: The paragraph begins: “However, there are two big problems… The ‘getting started’ problem … we are tribal creatures and conflicted about the ideals ourselves. We often ignore the suffering of strangers…”. The key point is that human weaknesses make it difficult to design AI with ideal ethical behaviour. Therefore D is correct.
Elimination: A (abuse of power) is not mentioned; B and C are incorrect.

Explanation for Questions 20–23 (YES / NO / NOT GIVEN)

20. YES.
Statement: Machines with the ability to make moral decisions may prevent us from promoting the interests of our communities.
Why: The passage states: “We might lose our freedom to discriminate in favour of our own communities, for example.”. This means that if AI maintains the moral high ground, it may prevent or limit us from prioritising our own communities’ interests. Therefore the statement matches the passage → YES.

21. NOT GIVEN.
Statement: Silicon police would need to exist in large numbers in order to be effective.
Why: The passage mentions the concept of “ethical silicon police” and states “They might be so good at doing it that we won't notice them”. However, it does not mention anything about how many would be needed or their scale of operation. Therefore there is no information → NOT GIVEN.

22. NO.
Statement: Many people are comfortable with the prospect of their independence being restricted by machines.
Why: The passage states: “few of us are likely to welcome such a future.”. This means most people would not be comfortable with such restrictions. Since the statement claims the opposite, the answer is → NO.

23. YES.
Statement: If we want to ensure that machines act in our best interests, we all need to work together.
Why: The conclusion states: “it will require a cooperative spirit, and a willingness to set aside self-interest.”. This clearly indicates that cooperation is necessary if we want AI to act in humanity’s best interests → YES.
