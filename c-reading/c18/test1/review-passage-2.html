Question 14–18

14. B — bad outcomes for a forest when people focus only on its financial reward
Explanation: Paragraph B describes the past situation when forest owners cared only about trees with high value (timber). This led to the practice of high-grading (cutting only the best trees) and leaving poor-quality ones behind. As a result: “these trees … compete for growth … shade out desirable regeneration and decrease the health of a stand leaving it more vulnerable to poor weather and disease.”
Summary: Paragraph B clearly shows the negative consequences of focusing only on financial value.

15. A — reference to the aspects of any tree that contribute to its worth
Explanation: Paragraph A directly lists the factors that determine a tree’s “value”: “species, size, form, condition, quality, function, and accessibility.” These clearly indicate the aspects that contribute to a tree’s worth.

16. C — mention of the potential use of wood to help run vehicles
Explanation: Paragraph C discusses wood energy and states that wood can be converted into liquid fuels such as “ethanol and gasoline for lorries and cars.” → this clearly indicates its use for transportation.

17. E — examples of insects that attack trees
Explanation: Paragraph E lists several pests/insects: “hemlock wooly adelgid, Asian longhorned beetle, emerald ash borer, and gypsy moth.” This paragraph focuses on insect damage and pruning methods to control them.

18. B — an alternative name for trees that produce low-use wood
Explanation: Paragraph B states: “Some people even call these ‘junk trees’.” — this is the alternative name used for trees that produce low-use wood.
