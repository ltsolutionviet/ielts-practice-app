10 — A (Chris Cheeseman)
Statement: It would be hard to create a construction alternative to concrete that offers so many comparable benefits.
Why: Chris Cheeseman (Sections B–C) emphasises: “Concrete is amazing stuff. Making anything with similar properties is going to be very difficult.” This clearly states that it would be difficult to produce another material with comparable advantages.

11 — B (Markus Mannström)
Statement: Worries about the environment have led to increased interest in wood as a construction material.
Why: Markus Mannström, Vice President at Stora Enso (Section F), says the company is seeing growing global demand for wood construction, with climate change concerns acting as the key driver. This means environmental concerns have increased interest in timber buildings.

12 — D (Felix Preston and Johanna Lehne)
Statement: Expense has been a factor in the negative response to the development of new cements.
Why: Section H (Preston & Lehne) states: “At present, these alternatives are rarely as cost-effective as conventional cement.” This shows that cost is an important factor limiting the adoption of alternative cement materials.

13 — A (Chris Cheeseman)
Statement: The environmental damage caused by concrete is due to it being produced in large quantities.
Why: In Section B, Cheeseman explains: “Cement is high carbon, but concrete is not. But it is the scale on which it is used that makes it high carbon. The sheer scale of manufacture is so huge, that is the issue.” In other words, the environmental impact results mainly from the enormous volume in which concrete is produced and used.
