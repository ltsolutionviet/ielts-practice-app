Questions 27–30 (Multiple choice)

27. The writer describes the Romeo and Juliet lesson in order to demonstrate

Answer: B – how a teacher handles a range of learning needs

Explanation:
The opening paragraph describes a classroom with students at very different levels — some can read Shakespeare directly, some rely on “No Fear Shakespeare”, and others need to draw in order to stay focused.
→ The purpose is to illustrate how a teacher must deal with and manage a wide range of student abilities within the same class.

28. What does the writer say about streaming in the third paragraph?

Answer: A – It has a very broad appeal

Explanation:
The final sentence of paragraph 3 states: “The practice is intuitively appealing to almost every stakeholder.”
→ This means that streaming (grouping students by ability) is supported by many different groups.

29. What idea is suggested by the reference to Mount Qomolangma in the fifth paragraph?

Answer: C – students not achieving their full potential

Explanation:
Paragraph 5 uses the image of climbing Mount Qomolangma (Everest):

“The brightest ones will never summit Mount Qomolangma, and the stragglers won’t enjoy the lovely stroll...”

This suggests that the strongest students may never reach the peak of their potential because they must move at the pace of weaker students.

30. What does the word 'scaffolding' in the sixth paragraph refer to?

Answer: C – the assistance given to a student in their initial stages of learning

Explanation:
In educational theory (particularly associated with Vygotsky), “scaffolding” refers to the support provided by teachers, materials, or examples that helps students learn something new, which is gradually removed once the learner can perform the task independently.
→ In other words, it refers to initial learning support.

Questions 31–35 (Summary Completion)

Reference section: From “Despite all this, there is limited empirical evidence...” to the end of paragraph 7.

31. there is very little indication that streaming leads to ...

Answer: H – higher achievements

“... there is limited empirical evidence to suggest that streaming results in better outcomes for students.”
→ There is no clear evidence that streaming improves academic achievement.

32. the most significant impact is on those students placed in the ...

Answer: D – bottom sets

“... streaming appears to significantly — and negatively — affect those students assigned to the lowest sets.”

33. especially where a large proportion of them have ...

Answer: F – disadvantaged backgrounds

“These students tend to have much higher representation of low socioeconomic class.”
→ This refers to students from disadvantaged backgrounds.

34. Meanwhile, for the ...

Answer: E – brightest pupils

“Less significant is the small benefit for those lucky clever students in the higher sets.”
→ This means high-achieving students gain only a small benefit.

35. teachers tend to have ...

Answer: B – lower expectations

“Streaming students by diagnosed achievement automatically limits what the teacher feels the student is capable of.”
→ Teachers tend to set lower expectations for students in lower groups.

Questions 36–40 (YES / NO / NOT GIVEN)

36. The Vygotsky model of education supports the concept of a mixed-ability class.

Answer: NO

Explanation:
Paragraph 6 states: “If we accept this model, it follows that streaming students with similar ZPDs would be an efficient and effective solution.”
→ According to this interpretation of Vygotsky’s theory, grouping students by similar ability (streaming) may actually be effective, which is the opposite of mixed-ability grouping.
→ Therefore the answer is NO.

37. Some teachers are uncertain about allowing students to take on MKO roles in the classroom.

Answer: NOT GIVEN

Explanation:
The passage mentions that students can take on the role of MKO (More Knowledgeable Other), but it does not mention whether teachers are uncertain or hesitant about this idea.
→ Therefore the answer is NOT GIVEN.

38. It can be rewarding to teach knowledge which you have only recently acquired.

Answer: YES

Explanation:
Paragraph 9 states: “There is also something exciting about passing on skills and knowledge that you yourself have just mastered — a certain pride and zeal...”
→ This clearly shows that teaching something you have recently learned can be an exciting and rewarding experience.
→ Therefore the answer is YES.

39. The priority should be to ensure that the highest-achieving students attain their goals.

Answer: NO

Explanation:
The final paragraph states: “... we need the many to flourish — not suffer at the expense of a few bright stars.”
→ The writer argues that education should benefit all students, not only the highest achievers.
→ Therefore the answer is NO.

40. Taking part in collaborative outdoor activities with teachers and classmates can improve student outcomes in the classroom.

Answer: NOT GIVEN

Explanation:
The final paragraph mentions climbing a mountain together with students and describes the experience as “challenging” and something that “we make together”. However, the passage does not state that this activity improves classroom learning outcomes.
→ Therefore the answer is NOT GIVEN.
